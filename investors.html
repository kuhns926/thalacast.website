The "Living Laboratory" Capital Stack

Zero Technology Risk. 100% Integration.

ThalaCast is a shovel-ready infrastructure project. We do not rely on experimental physics to achieve municipal resilience. Every component of our city—from the Iron-Air battery vaults to the ZLD crystallizers and vacuum sewers—is commercially available technology. Our proprietary value is in the symbiotic integration of these systems.

Because ThalaCast acts as a real-world blueprint for solving drought, power grid failure, and zero-emission housing, we utilize a dual-pronged Capital Stack: securing non-dilutive government grants (DOE, EPA, HUD) to fund the heavy utility infrastructure, significantly de-risking the real estate and AI compute components for our private equity partners.

OpEx: The $5M "Zero-Utility" Run Rate

Traditional municipalities and data centers are crippled by variable utility costs. ThalaCast generates 100% of its own power, distills its own water, and processes its own waste on-site. Therefore, our municipal utility exposure is zero.

Because the city is highly automated, we operate on an incredibly lean municipal workforce of just 49 highly skilled specialists. Our total baseline operating cost (OpEx)—including comprehensive payroll, benefits, and hardware maintenance—is capped at roughly $5.0M per year.

This lean operational profile guarantees that once a fraction of our housing or AI compute capacity is leased, the entire city breaks even, turning the remaining capacity into pure profit.
